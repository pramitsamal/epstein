Before I arrived in Iraq, I had thought there might be behind-the-
scenes negotiations going on to extend the Status of Forces
Agreement to allow some troops to remain behind. But after spending
several days talking with Iraqi and American officials, civilian and
military, I came to the conclusion that no talks had started because
each side was waiting for the other to go first. Mr. Gates has finally
broken through the After you, Alphonse syndrome, but his
intervention may be too little, too late.

Mr. Boot is a senior fellow in national security studies at the Council
on Foreign Relations.
