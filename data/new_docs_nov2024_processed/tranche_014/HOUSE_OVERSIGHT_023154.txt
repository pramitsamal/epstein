29 August 1994

Fall of the Berlin Wall.

The Arab League decides to return its headquarters to Cairo.
President G.H. Bush suspends dialogue with the PLO.

Iraq invades Kuwait.

The US launches Operation Desert Storm to liberate Kuwait.

The Lebanese parliament orders the disbanding of all militias by this date.
Hezbollah remains active and the South Lebanon Army (SLA) refuses to
disband.

The Lebanese parliament grants an amnesty for all crimes committed
during the civil war, 1975-90.

Invitation to the Madrid Peace Conference. The US letters of assurance to
the Palestinians and Israelis.

The Middle East Peace Conference opens in Madrid. Face-to-face
bilateral talks for the first time between Israeli and Arab delegations, as
well as with a joint Palestinian-Jordanian delegation.

UN General Assembly Resolution 46/86 (Zionism-Racism).
Formal dissolution of the USSR.

Israeli Labor Party wins the elections. Yitzhak Rabin becomes Prime
Minister.

Secret Israeli-PLO talks begin in Oslo, and develop into full-scale official
negotiations in May that year.

Secret signing of the Oslo Accords in Oslo by Israeli Foreign Minister
Peres, Palestinian Chief Negotiator Qorei and Norwegian Foreign
Minister Holst.

Israel and the PLO exchange letters of recognition.

Signature of the Declaration of Principles on Interim Self- Government
Agreements in Washington.

Israel-Jordan Common Agenda.

Letter from Israel's Foreign Minister Peres to Norway's Foreign Minister
Holst on the Status of East Jerusalem.

Establishment of the AHLC.

An Israeli opens fire at the Ibrahimi Mosque or Tomb of the Patriarchs in
Hebron, killing 29 Palestinians.

UN Security Council Resolution 904 on the Hebron Massacre.

Protocol on Economic Relations between the State of Israel and the PLO
(Paris Protocol).

Gaza-Jericho Agreement.

The IDF withdraws from Gaza city and Jericho.
Yasir Arafat arrives in Gaza.

Washington Declaration (Jordan-Israel peace talks).

Agreement on Preparatory Transfer of Power and Responsibilities.
