NCT’s Board Members (The Nobel Family Society)

The Nobel Family Society is a private society of which only the descendants of Immanuel
Nobel, father of Robert, Ludvig and Alfred Nobel, are eligible as members. The 4 Nobels of
the Nobel Family Society below are NCT’s founders and board members.

yN

Michael Nobel Born in 1940

Chairman of NCT Asia, Former
Chairman of NCT Switzerland
2007-2009/former Chairman of the
Board of Directors of the Nobel
Family Society /former Chairman
of the Appeal of the Peace Prize
Laureates Foundation. Michael
obtained his PH D. in pyscho-
pedagogy from the University of
Lausanne. He is knowledgeable in
the fields of economics and
environmentology, has deep ties
with Japanese Universities and
has been honored with many
awards.

Nobel Family Society Members

Gustaf Nobel Born in1950

Appointed Chairman of NCT in
Switzerland in Jan.2009.. After
studies at University of Lund,
Sweden, in marketing and
economics he started his career as
an international businessman
working in more than 15 countries
all over the world. Over the last 8
years he has become very much
involved in environmental projects
and renewable energy solutions,
as well as recycling of refuse, and
is an authority in the field.

Peter Nobel Born in 1953

Trustee of NCT in Switzerland.
Peter is a graduate with MSc in
Material Science and Engineering
from The Royal Institute in
Stockholm. Peter is a well-known
businessman and entrepreneur. A
major part of his experience relates
to energy associating product
programs, product and technology
development activities targeting

environmental concerned segments.

Peter works extensively with
supporting clean technologies for
environmental improvements and
energy saving programs.

Philip Nobel Born in 1970

Trustee of NCT in Switzerland.

In Switzerland Philip studied
marketing and economics. He
then moved to the Bahamas in
2006 and is currently actively
involved in environmental issues
all throughout the Caribbean
islands; he’s an active member of
the Bahamas National Trust as
well as the Nature Conservancy-
group whose aims are to preserve
and to improve the natural
environment of the islands and its
surrounding waters.
