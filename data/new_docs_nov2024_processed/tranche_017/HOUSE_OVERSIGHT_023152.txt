Yasir Arafat, leader of the Fatah Party, elected chairman of the PLO.

President Nasser denounces the 1967 war cease-fire, marking the begin-

ning of the War of Attrition against Israel.

Creation of the Organization of the Islamic Conference (OIC).

Cairo Agreement.

Rogers Plan.

War of Attrition cease-fire.

‘Black September’ in Jordan. The PLO is expelled from Jordan.

President Gamal Abdel Nasser dies and is replaced by Vice President

Anwar al-Sadat.

Syria’s Minister of Defense, Hafez al-Assad, leads the “Correctionist

Movement’ military coup.

Hafez al-Assad is elected President of Syria in a referendum.

October or Yom Kippur War. Egypt and Syria attack Israeli forces in the

Sinai and the Golan Heights.
OAPEC decides on an oil embargo.
UN Security Council Resolution 338.

First Egyptian-Israeli Disengagement of Forces Agreement.

Israel-Syria Separation of Forces Agreement.

Arab summit at Rabat declares the PLO the ‘sole legitimate representative

of the Palestinian people’.

Arab League Resolution on Palestine Liberation Organization.
PLO Chairman Arafat addresses the UN General Assembly in Geneva.
UN General Assembly Resolutions 3236 and 3237 (PLO).

Beginning of the Lebanese Civil War.

Second Egyptian-Israel Disengagement of Forces Agreement.
UN General Assembly Resolution 3379 (Zionism-Racism).

Lebanese President Suleiman Frangieh invites Syrian troops into the

country.

Menachem Begin’s Likud Party wins Israeli national elections after 29

years of Labor Party dominance.

In a speech in Cairo, President Anwar al-Sadat announces his readiness to

travel to Israel.

Anwar al-Sadat and Menachem Begin speeches to the Knesset in Jerusalem.

Prime Minister Begin’s Knesset speech on Home Rule.

Operation Litany—Israel invades South Lebanon.

UN Security Council Resolutions 425 and 426 (Lebanon).

Camp David Accords.

Arab League Summit in Baghdad. Egypt suspended from the Organiza-

tion. Its headquarters to be moved to Tunis.
