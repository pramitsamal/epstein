Overview

The Las Vegas Strip has historically outperformed competitive U.S. retail markets and continues to experience very strong absorption of
new construction, low vacancy rates, and an abundance of retail tenants seeking to access high-traffic pedestrian locations. These factors
together with controlled levels of new development will cause significant long-term rent increases across the submarket.
